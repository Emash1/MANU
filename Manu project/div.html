Marcus Aurelius Antoninus was emperor of Rome from 161 AD until his death in 180.
The last in a series of rulers that historians now refer to as the Five Good Emperors, he was chosen as imperial heir when he was still a child.
Raised with his future job in mind, Aurelius was pulled out of the empire’s questionable public school system and educated at home by Greek tutors and Stoic philosophers.

As intended, this world-class education ended up having a positive influence over Marcus Aurelius’ reign.
His decisions were informed not by lust or jealousy or greed — as had been the case for many Julio-Claudian emperors — but by his deep understanding of law and logic.
